The Top 10 NFL Safeties of All Time

September 2024 Â· 4 minute read

When discussing the greatest safeties in NFL history, several legendary players come to mind. From their exceptional ability to defend against both the pass and the run to their game-changing impact on the field, these players have left an indelible mark on the sport. Let's delve into the top 10 NFL safeties of all time, showcasing their remarkable careers and contributions to the game.

1. Ronnie Lott

Ronnie Lott, known for his hard-hitting style and exceptional coverage skills, is widely regarded as one of the greatest safeties in NFL history. A key member of the San Francisco 49ers and later the Los Angeles Raiders, Lott's career was defined by his fierce competitiveness and leadership on the field. With 63 interceptions and 730 tackles over his illustrious career, Lott's impact on the game remains unparalleled.

2. Ed Reed

Ed Reed's instinctive playmaking abilities and ball-hawking skills solidify his position as one of the top safeties in NFL history. Spending the majority of his career with the Baltimore Ravens, Reed's 64 career interceptions and 1,590 interception return yards showcase his exceptional talent as a game-changer in the secondary. His ability to read quarterbacks and make electrifying plays earned him numerous accolades and a spot in the Hall of Fame.

3. Troy Polamalu

Troy Polamalu's dynamic playing style and unparalleled football instincts make him a standout figure in the history of NFL safeties. As a Pittsburgh Steeler, Polamalu's impact went beyond statistics, as his disruptive presence often dictated opposing offenses' game plans. With 32 career interceptions and 12 forced fumbles, Polamalu's ability to impact games in multiple ways cements his legacy as one of the all-time greats.

4. Kenny Easley

Kenny Easley's dominance during his tenure with the Seattle Seahawks earned him a reputation as one of the most feared safeties in NFL history. Easley's physicality and exceptional coverage skills allowed him to amass 32 interceptions and five All-Pro selections during his relatively short career. Despite his career being cut short due to health issues, Easley's impact on the game cannot be overstated.

5. Paul Krause

Paul Krause's remarkable consistency and durability set him apart as one of the premier safeties in NFL history. Spending the majority of his career with the Minnesota Vikings, Krause's record 81 career interceptions stands as a testament to his exceptional ball skills and impact on the game. His ability to read quarterbacks and create turnovers remains unmatched.

6. Brian Dawkins

Brian Dawkins' passion, intensity, and playmaking ability make him a standout figure in the pantheon of NFL safeties. Dawkins' 37 interceptions, 26 forced fumbles, and 1,131 tackles reflect his dynamic impact on the Philadelphia Eagles' defense. Known for his highlight-reel hits and leadership on and off the field, Dawkins' legacy as a game-changing safety is firmly established.

7. Ken Houston

Ken Houston's remarkable career as a safety for the Houston Oilers and Washington Redskins solidifies his place among the all-time greats. Houston's 49 interceptions and 12 Pro Bowl selections underscore his exceptional consistency and impact as a defensive stalwart. His ability to control the secondary and make crucial plays in key moments solidifies his status as a legendary safety.

8. Emlen Tunnell

Emlen Tunnell's pioneering career as one of the first African American players in the NFL and his exceptional play at the safety position make him an iconic figure in the history of the sport. Tunnell's 79 career interceptions and his impact as a trailblazer for future generations of players cement his legacy as a true pioneer and one of the greatest safeties in NFL history.

9. Steve Atwater

Steve Atwater's hard-hitting style and exceptional play in both pass coverage and run support distinguish him as one of the premier safeties in NFL history. Atwater's impact with the Denver Broncos, highlighted by 24 interceptions and over 1,000 tackles, solidifies his reputation as a game-changing force in the secondary.

10. Rod Woodson

Rod Woodson's versatility and playmaking abilities, showcased during his tenure with the Pittsburgh Steelers and later with the Baltimore Ravens, make him a standout figure among the greatest NFL safeties. Woodson's 71 career interceptions, exceptional coverage skills, and impact as a return specialist underscore his influence on the game and secure his place among the top safeties of all time.

The top 10 NFL safeties of all time have left an indelible mark on the sport through their exceptional skill, leadership, and game-changing impact. Their contributions have not only shaped the position of safety but have also redefined the way defense is played in the NFL, leaving a lasting legacy for future generations of players to emulate.
